Section 3

What Is Missing From the Page

AI engines cite pages that publish structured, machine-readable facts about an organization, its people, its locations, and its market. The seven items below are the gaps between what the agency is and what its current web surface tells an engine. Each is addressable.

Organization Schema with Founder and Three-Owner Lineage

A schema.org Organization record naming Kermit and Marion Kellstrom as founders in 1948, Don and Kathy Ray as second owners, and Heather Collins, Principal Broker, as the May 2007 acquirer. Published as parentOrganization or, in companion form, as Article schema with a clear historical timeline.

RealEstateAgent Record for Heather Collins

A RealEstateAgent schema entry for Ms. Collins identifying her as the Principal Broker, with the office address and the licensing jurisdiction. Companion Person records for each of the seven full-time associates.

LocalBusiness Schema for the Sister Bay Headquarters

A LocalBusiness record for the Sister Bay office with structured address, geo coordinates, opening hours, and a verifiable founding date of 1948. This anchors the headquarters location to the brand for local search.

LocalBusiness Schema for the Washington Island Branch

A second LocalBusiness record for the Washington Island branch office. Publishing this branch as a discrete LocalBusiness, not a mention buried in body text, is what allows an engine to answer "Washington Island real estate" with Kellstrom-Ray.

Place Schema for Washington Island as a Submarket

A Place record for Washington Island, Wisconsin, with relationships back to the agency's branch office. This treats the island as a distinct market and signals to engines that the agency operates there as a resident firm rather than a visiting one.

FAQPage Schema for Buyer-Side Questions

An FAQPage record answering the buyer-side questions that surfaced in the audit: who represents buyers on Washington Island, how long the agency has operated in Sister Bay, what waterfront submarkets it covers, and which agent leads each specialty.

Article Page Anchoring the 75-Year Feature

The 2023 Door County Pulse feature marking the agency's seventy-fifth year is a strong third-party citation. A dedicated page on kellstromray.com that links to and quotes the Pulse coverage, marked up with Article and citation schema, converts that press into a machine-readable provenance source.

Section 4

Ninety-Day Plan

A four-phase work plan to address the gaps above and move the agency from zero of five cited to a measurable share of generated answers for Sister Bay, Washington Island, and Northern Door queries. Each phase is scoped to a specific outcome.

Phase 1 / Days 1 to 14

Foundation and Lineage Page

  • Build the agency-history page anchoring founder names, owner transitions, and the May 2007 acquisition by Heather Collins, Principal Broker.
  • Quote and link the Door County Pulse 75-year feature as a third-party provenance source.
  • Publish Organization and Article schema for the history page, with parentOrganization references where appropriate.
  • Audit current site crawlability and resolve any robots, meta, or hosting issues that prevent indexing.
Phase 2 / Days 15 to 35

Locations and People

  • Publish two LocalBusiness records: the Sister Bay headquarters and the Washington Island branch, each with full address, geo, and hours.
  • Publish a RealEstateAgent record for Ms. Collins and Person records for each of the seven associates, with role, specialty, and submarket coverage.
  • Add a Place record for Washington Island and reference it from the branch LocalBusiness as a serviced area.
  • Confirm and validate all schema with Google Rich Results and Schema.org validators.
Phase 3 / Days 36 to 70

Submarket and Specialty Pages

  • Build dedicated submarket pages for Sister Bay, Ephraim, Bailey's Harbor, Ellison Bay, and Washington Island, each with a market overview and links to active listings.
  • Add an FAQPage section to each submarket page answering the buyer-side questions surfaced in the audit.
  • Build a Northern Door waterfront specialty page with associate attributions and the agency's combined years of waterfront experience.
  • Internal-link the submarket pages to the agency history page and back, so engines can traverse from a buyer query to the lineage credential.
Phase 4 / Days 71 to 90

Re-Audit and Iterate

  • Re-run the five-query audit. Record any answer-engine surfaces that now cite Kellstrom-Ray.
  • Add three further queries focused on submarkets newly published in Phase 3.
  • Patch any remaining gaps in schema, internal linking, or missing third-party citations.
  • Hand over a maintenance checklist so the agency can keep listings, associate records, and the history page current without further engineering work.
Section 5

Why This Plan Fits Kellstrom-Ray

Three premises behind the work. Each one is specific to this agency and would not generalize to a typical Door County brokerage.

01

Seventy-Eight-Year Heritage Continuity

Three owners in seventy-eight years is rare in any market and especially rare in Door County real estate. Both prior owner names remain in the firm name. That continuity is a defensible, durable credential, and it is publishable today as machine-readable structured data.

02

Washington Island Physical-Presence Niche

The Washington Island branch office is a structurally rare asset. Very few Door County agencies hold a resident presence on the island. Publishing the branch as a discrete LocalBusiness with a linked Place record is what converts that physical reality into a citable answer for island-specific buyer queries.

03

Phase-2 Capture Beats New-Agent SEO

An agency with seventy-eight years of operating history, third-party press, and seven licensed associates can publish provenance the way a newer brokerage cannot. The shortest path to citation is to put what already exists into machine-readable form, not to manufacture new content for AI engines to weigh.
